About Mercury

  • Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system with a diameter of 3,031 miles.
  • It is the closest planet to the Sun at about 36 million miles away.
  • Mercury has a very thin atmosphere made up of atoms blasted off its surface.
  • The surface is heavily cratered, similar to the moon.
  • Craters on Mercury are named after famous artists, musicians and authors.
  • Mercury has no moons or rings.
  • Mariner 10 was the first spacecraft to visit Mercury in 1974-75.
  • NASA's MESSENGER probe mapped 100% of Mercury's surface from 2004-2015.
  • Temperatures range from -290°F to 800°F due to varying distances from the Sun.
  • Mercury has cliff-like features called Scarps likely caused by the planet shrinking.

    About Venus

  • Venus is the 2nd planet from the Sun and Earth's closest neighbor at 25 million miles away.
  • Venus is around the same size as Earth with a diameter of 7,520 miles.
  • The surface temperature on Venus reaches 880°F, making it the hottest planet.
  • Thick clouds of sulfuric acid trap heat and create a strong greenhouse effect.
  • Venus has continent-like highlands and plains covered in craters and volcanoes.
  • Venus rotates backwards compared to most planets, called retrograde rotation.
  • Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.
  • Venus has no natural moons or rings.
  • The Soviet Venera probes first landed on Venus in the 1970s.
  • NASA's Magellan probe mapped 98% of Venus' surface with radar in the 1990s.

  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet to harbor life.
  • The diameter of Earth is approximately 7,917 miles (12,742 km).
  • Earth's surface is divided into several rigid tectonic plates that migrate across the surface over many millions of years.
  • Earth has one natural satellite, the Moon, which orbits around it.
  • Earth has a strong magnetic field created by the rotation of its liquid iron core.
  • Earth's atmosphere consists mostly of nitrogen and oxygen which allow life to thrive.
  • Earth is the only planet in our solar system not named after a Greek or Roman deity.
  • The highest point on Earth is Mount Everest at 29,031 feet (8,848 meters).

    About Mars

  • Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest planet in our solar system.
  • The diameter of Mars is approximately 4,220 miles (6,790 km).
  • Mars is known as the "Red Planet" due to the iron oxide that gives its surface a reddish appearance.
  • Mars has two small irregularly shaped moons called Phobos and Deimos.
  • Some key surface features on Mars are Olympus Mons, the largest volcano, and Valles Marineris, the largest canyon.
  • Mars has an extremely thin atmosphere made up mostly of carbon dioxide, nitrogen and argon.
  • Mars has polar ice caps that expand and contract with the seasons.
  • Evidence of ancient rivers and oceans suggests Mars may have once supported life.

    About Jupiter

  • Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest planet in our solar system.
  • The diameter of Jupiter is about 88,846 miles (142,984 km), over 11 times larger than Earth.
  • Jupiter has at least 79 confirmed moons, including the four large Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede and Callisto.
  • Jupiter has three faint rings composed of small dust grains from space debris and asteroids.
  • The Great Red Spot is a huge, long-lasting storm on Jupiter about 1.3 times as wide as Earth.
  • Jupiter rotates very rapidly, completing one rotation in just under 10 hours.
  • Jupiter emits more energy than it receives from the Sun through a process called radioactive self-heating.
  • NASA space probes like Pioneer 10, Voyager, Galileo, Cassini and Juno have done flybys or orbited Jupiter.

    About Saturn

  • Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second largest planet after Jupiter.
  • Saturn has a diameter of about 72,367 miles (116,464 km), over 9 times larger than Earth.
  • Saturn is a gas giant made up mostly of hydrogen and helium.
  • Saturn has the most extensive and complex ring system of any planet in our solar system.
  • Saturn has 82 confirmed moons, the largest being Titan and Rhea.
  • Saturn is the flattest planet, its polar diameter being 90% of its equatorial diameter due to its low density.
  • Saturn has the fastest winds in the solar system, reaching speeds of over 1,100 mph (1,800 km/h).
  • Saturn orbits the Sun once every 29.4 years and completes one rotation roughly every 10.5 hours.

    About Uranus

  • Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third largest planet in our solar system.
  • Uranus has a diameter of about 31,518 miles (50,724 km), four times larger than Earth.
  • Unlike other planets, Uranus rotates nearly on its side in relation to its orbit around the Sun.
  • Uranus is an ice giant composed mainly of water, ammonia and methane surrounding a rocky core.
  • Uranus has 27 known moons, including Titania and Oberon, and a complex ring system.
  • Uranus takes about 84 years to orbit the Sun and takes about 17 hours to rotate once on its axis.
  • The atmosphere of Uranus contains hydrogen, helium and methane which gives it a blue-green tint.

  • Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in our solar system.
  • Neptune has a diameter of about 30,599 miles (49,244 km), making it the smallest of the gas giants.
  • Neptune orbits the Sun once every 164.8 years and has a rotation period of 19 hours.
  • Neptune has a thick atmosphere made up predominantly of hydrogen and helium.
  • Neptune has 14 confirmed moons, the largest being Triton.
  • Neptune has a very active climate with supersonic winds and massive storms like the Great Dark Spot.
  • Neptune has a ring system composed of five main rings rich in ice particles and dust.
  • Neptune's blue color comes from small amounts of methane gas in its atmosphere.
